Marketing Strategies

Effective approaches to grow your business online and offline

ONLINE STRATEGIES

Digital approaches to reach and engage your audience

1. Local SEO & Google My Business

How it works: Optimizes your online presence so nearby customers find you on Google.

Helps by: Improving local search rankings and visibility.

Example: A bakery updates its Google profile with photos, hours, and reviews, and shows up first when someone searches "bakery near me."

2. Referral Programs

How it works: Reward existing customers for bringing in new ones.

Helps by: Turning loyal customers into brand ambassadors.

Example: A salon offers a 10% discount if you refer a friend who books a service.

3. Influencer Marketing

How it works: Partner with local influencers to promote your product.

Helps by: Reaching a larger or niche audience through trusted voices.

Example: A local fashion store sends outfits to a popular Instagrammer for a review.

4. Loyalty Programs

How it works: Offer rewards for repeat purchases.

Helps by: Encouraging return visits and boosting customer retention.

Example: A coffee shop gives a free drink after every 7 purchases.

OFFLINE STRATEGIES

Traditional approaches to connect with your local community

1. Exclusive Member Cards or VIP Access

How it works: Provide special perks or early access to loyal customers.

Helps by: Creating a sense of exclusivity and stronger customer loyalty.

Example: A clothing store lets VIP members shop a sale one day early.

2. Street Signage & Window Displays

How it works: Use eye-catching visuals to attract walk-in traffic.

Helps by: Making your storefront stand out and grabbing attention.

Example: A bookstore uses chalkboard signs with witty quotes and weekly deals.

3. Flyers, Brochures & Door Hangers

How it works: Distribute printed marketing in your local area.

Helps by: Reaching nearby households or foot traffic directly.

Example: A cleaning service leaves door hangers in nearby neighborhoods.

4. Sponsor Local Events or Teams

How it works: Support a local cause or event in exchange for branding.

Helps by: Increasing brand visibility and goodwill in the community.

Example: A restaurant sponsors a kids' soccer team—logo appears on jerseys.

5. Host Workshops or Demonstrations

How it works: Offer hands-on experiences related to your product or service.

Helps by: Building trust and showcasing expertise.

Example: A gardening store hosts a free composting demo on weekends.

6. Branded Merchandise & Referral Cards

How it works: Distribute items with your logo or referral info.

Helps by: Keeping your brand in sight and encouraging word-of-mouth.

Example: A gym hands out water bottles with referral codes printed on them.
